Creatine is known for building muscle and strength. However, creatine has also many benefits for the brain, such as improved cognition, better memory, reduced sleep demand, and enhanced glucose tolerance. Creatine maintains intracellular ATP in the brain, and the brain consumes 20% of your total energy per day. Supplementing creatine enhances brain creatine stores. Many neurodegenerative disorders are linked low brain creatine levels, often in a dose specific manner. Creatine improves cognition and memory, especially in the elderly or when you're under metabolic stress. Supplementing creatine improves recovery from traumatic brain injury. Thanks to its effects on the brain, creatine supplementation may reduce sleep need and sleep pressure. Loading up on creatine before sleep deprivation maintains better cognition and skill performance.
