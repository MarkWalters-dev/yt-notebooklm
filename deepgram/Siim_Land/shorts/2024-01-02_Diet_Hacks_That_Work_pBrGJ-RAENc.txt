Dieting is one of those things where there are no shortcuts. You just have to find a way to make it easier for yourself. However, there are some things that make it easier. Number one, exercise before eating. Moving around obviously burns calories. However, exercise also makes you very insulin sensitive, which which lowers your glucose response after eating. Number two, walk after meals. Walking after meals has been seen to cut the blood sugar response in half. It also improves digestion. Number three, stop eating at 80% full. If you eat until 100%, you'll be stuffed and you'll probably overeat calories. If you eat until 80% full, you're going to be 100% in thirty minutes. This helps to prevent overeating. Number four, eat protein and fiber first. These are the most seed cheating food groups. High protein and fiber intake is linked to weight loss and keeping the weight off. Start your meals with protein and fiber before eating carbs to also keep your blood sugar more stable. And number five, don't eat too fast. This prevents getting stuffed and improves digestion.
