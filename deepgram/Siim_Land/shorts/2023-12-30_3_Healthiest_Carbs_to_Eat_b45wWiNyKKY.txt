Carbohydrates are thought to be bad for you. However, the research shows that people eating moderate amount of carbs live longer and have lower risk of mortality. Here's a list of the three healthiest carbohydrates you can eat. Number one, sweet potatoes, especially purple sweet potatoes. Sweet potatoes are a lower glycemic carb that's high in beta carotene. Dietary intake and circulating levels of beta carotene are inversely associated with all cause mortality. Purple sweet potatoes also contain anthocyanins, which have been found to be associated with lower disease and better eye health. Number two, buckwheat is another low glycemic cereal. Eating buckwheat is linked to lower blood pressure, lower cholesterol, and better other risk factors of cardiovascular disease. Buckwheat is also a good source of harder to get minerals such as copper and manganese. Number three, quinoa, another low glycemic grain, has been found to lower risk factors of heart disease in humans. Eating whole grains is consistently been shown to be linked to lower risk of mortality and heart disease mortality. You don't have to be afraid of carbohydrates.
