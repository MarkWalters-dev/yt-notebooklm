### Methodological Philosophy
*   **Spectrum of Scientific Rigor:** Acknowledges the differences in experimental rigor across disciplines:
    *   **Mathematics:** Axiomatic systems that are directly applicable to reality.
    *   **Physics:** Testable hypotheses and repeatable experiments.

### Data Collection and Analysis
*   **Comprehensive Tracking:** Records all aspects of life, including:
    *   Dietary intake (weighing all food).
    *   Supplement intake.
    *   Exercise routines.
    *   Sleep patterns.
    *   Biomarkers (blood tests, heart rate variability (HRV), resting heart rate, etc.).
*   **Correlation-Based Analysis:**
    *   Aligns input data (diet, exercise, sleep) with biomarker results from blood tests.
    *   Uses a correlation-based approach to identify potential relationships between inputs and outputs.
    *   Initially uses unadjusted correlations, then applies multivariate adjusted linear regression models for deeper analysis as data accumulates.
*   **Iterative Refinement:** Continuously tests interventions, re-evaluates data, and adjusts the approach based on observed correlations and biomarker responses.
*   **Intervention Strategy:**
    *   Identifies multiple potential interventions (4-5) that correlate with a biomarker improvement.
    *   Tests these interventions concurrently.
    *   Tracks biomarker changes in subsequent tests to confirm sustained positive effects or identify weakening correlations.
*   **Protocol:**
    1.  **Weigh all food and track supplement intake.**
    2.  **Track daily physiological data:** Heart rate variability (HRV), resting heart rate, sleep quality/duration.
    3.  **Obtain regular blood tests** (e.g., every 50 days).
    4.  **Align input data** (diet, supplements, HRV, sleep) with blood test results for the period preceding each test.
    5.  **After two tests:** Perform two-sample t-tests to identify significant differences in inputs and outputs.
    6.  **After three or more tests:**
        *   Use simple correlation analysis to identify potential relationships.
        *   For variables with extensive historical data (e.g., HRV, resting heart rate), use multivariate adjusted linear regression models, adjusting for known strong confounders like body weight.
    7.  **Identify strong correlations** (e.g., 10-20 significant correlations with a biomarker).
    8.  **Test interventions** based on these correlations.
    9.  **Re-evaluate data** with subsequent tests to confirm sustained improvements or identify weakening correlations.
    10. **Iteratively refine** diet, supplements, and lifestyle based on the ongoing analysis.
*   **Focus on Net Positive Outcome:** Assesses the overall impact of a diet or intervention across a wide range of biomarkers and organ systems, not just isolated metrics.
