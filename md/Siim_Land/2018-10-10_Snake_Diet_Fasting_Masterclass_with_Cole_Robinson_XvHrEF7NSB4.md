*   **Thyroid Hormones:** Blood test results indicating slightly low T3 hormone should be secondary to how an individual feels. If feeling "great" after significant weight loss, minor deviations from reference ranges may not be clinically significant.

*   **Indicators of True Metabolic Health:**
    *   **Recovery Time:** A healthy individual can rapidly recover normal blood sugar levels after a high sugar intake (e.g., from 10 mmol/L to 4.4 mmol/L within an hour).

## Dry Fasting

*   **Concept:** Abstaining from both food and water.
*   **Aggressiveness:** More aggressive for weight loss and detoxification than water fasting.
*   **Metabolic Water Production:** A deep dry-fasted state stimulates the body to produce metabolic water from fat stores (similar to a camel), enhancing "water adaptation." This allows for quicker entry into fat-burning mode when the body senses a lack of exogenous water.
*   **Duration:** Can only be sustained for shorter periods than water fasts (e.g., 5-7 days for an individual with sufficient body fat).
*   **Cycling:** For very long fasts, cycling between dry fasting (as long as tolerable) and hydrating with Snake Juice is recommended.
*   **Initial Adaptation:** First dry fasts may induce severe headaches (e.g., at 48 hours) as the body shifts to metabolic water production, similar to the "hunger phase" during the transition to ketosis.

## Carb Cycling for Performance and Lean Maintenance

*   **Principle:** A protocol for lean individuals focusing on combining fasting with specific zero-carb and carb refeed days.
*   **Benefits:**
    *   Maintains highly stable blood sugar and insulin sensitivity.
    *   Maximizes growth hormone and testosterone production.
    *   Allows effective "pounding" of nutrients during refeeds due to heightened insulin sensitivity, leading to better muscle gains and explosive performance.
    *   **Dual Fuel System in Action:**
        *   **Fasted (Ketosis + Glycogen Stores):** Low-intensity activity (e.g., walking) primarily burns body fat.
        *   **Anaerobic Activity (Glycogen Access):** High-intensity bursts (e.g., sprints) utilize readily available muscle glycogen.
*   **Performance Differences (Carnivore vs. Carb Cycling):**
    *   Strict zero-carb carnivore can lead to feelings of flatness and reduced explosive strength in the gym, hindering optimal muscle gain.
    *   Carb cycling provides the necessary glycogen for peak performance and strength gains.
