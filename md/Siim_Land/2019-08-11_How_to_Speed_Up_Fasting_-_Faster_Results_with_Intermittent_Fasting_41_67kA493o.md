# 2019-08-11 How to Speed Up Fasting - Faster Results with Intermittent Fasting

**Executive Summary:**
*   Fasting benefits such as autophagy, fat burning, and stem cell activation typically require a minimum of 20-24 hours of fasting for significant metabolic shifts.
*   The "fasted state" is characterized by dropping glucose/insulin, increased fatty acid oxidation and ketone production, suppression of anabolic pathways (mTOR, IGF-1), and activation of catabolic pathways (AMPK, autophagy).
*   Metabolic "hacks" like exercise (endurance and resistance), heat/cold exposure, dietary polyphenols, medicinal mushrooms, and being in a state of ketosis can mimic fasted physiology and activate key longevity pathways (e.g., PGC-1 alpha, sirtuins, Nrf2), thereby accelerating the benefits of fasting.
*   While these hacks can speed up metabolic effects, a 'pure fasted state'—defined by metabolic suspension and the absence of food digestion—is a unique characteristic only achievable through actual fasting.
*   Daily time-restricted eating (e.g., a 16-hour fast) is crucial for overall metabolic health, digestion, and circadian rhythms, but often requires the application of these "hacks" to significantly induce deep longevity pathways like autophagy within shorter fasting windows.

## The Fasted State

A fasted state is distinct from a fed one, characterized by unique metabolic shifts that occur approximately 4-6 hours after the last calorie consumption, once food digestion is complete.

**Hallmark Features of a Fasted State:**
*   **Blood Glucose & Insulin:** Levels drop due to the absence of carbohydrates in the bloodstream.
*   **Fatty Acid Oxidation:** Increases as the body mobilizes stored adipose tissue for energy.
*   **Ketone Body Production:** Begins as the liver converts fatty acids into ketones.
    *   Fatty Acids -> Liver Conversion -> Ketone Bodies
    *   Ketone bodies then substitute for glucose in the brain and other vital organs.
*   **Anabolic Pathway Suppression:** Pathways like mTOR and IGF-1 are suppressed, as the body lacks nutrients for cell growth, maintaining energy efficiency.
*   **Catabolic Pathway Activation:** Pathways like AMPK and autophagy are activated.
    *   AMPK is activated under energy stress, promoting fatty acid oxidation, ketogenesis, gluconeogenesis, and autophagy.
*   **Mitochondrial Enhancement:** Metabolic factors such as PGC-1 alpha, sirtuins, phosphoproteins, and AMPK increase, promoting mitochondrial fusion and biogenesis, leading to more energy-efficient and abundant mitochondria.
*   **Antioxidant Defense:** Systems like Nrf2 and glutathione are activated, promoting detoxification and removal of free radicals.
*   **NAD+ Increase:** NAD+, a coenzyme vital for molecular reactions including energy production, mitochondrial function, DNA repair, and lifespan, increases.

Generally, 20-24 hours of fasting are required for a significant increase in powerful metabolic factors like autophagy, sirtuins, and glutathione. Shorter fasts (14-20 hours) primarily focus on insulin management and meal timing for convenience.

## Accelerating Fasting Benefits

Various activities and dietary compounds can activate the same metabolic pathways and mimic the physiology of a fasted state, allowing for faster achievement of its benefits.

**Methods to Mimic Fasted Physiology:**
*   **Exercise:** Physical activity lowers insulin, increases fat oxidation, and promotes AMPK activation due to liver glycogen depletion, which in turn leads to autophagy.
    *   Moderate intensity endurance exercise promotes PGC-1 alpha, leading to mitochondrial biogenesis and ketosis.
    *   Resistance training activates autophagy and reduces muscle cell keratosis. While resistance training itself doesn't activate mTOR or insulin during the exercise, it can prime the mTOR complex for activation post-fast upon refeeding.
    *   Prolonged exercise (e.g., two hours at moderate intensity) stimulates muscles to extract ketones from the blood.
*   **Heat Exposure:**
    *   Heat shock proteins promote autophagy and have neuroprotective effects.
    *   Saunas improve cardiovascular health and reduce all-cause mortality.
*   **Cold Exposure:** Lower temperatures activate FOXO proteins, which promote longevity.
*   **Dietary Polyphenols:** Found in herbal teas, coffee, dark leafy vegetables, dark chocolate, olive oil, chokeberries, and blueberries. These compounds stimulate autophagy, protect against neurodegeneration, and support the body's antioxidant system.
*   **Medicinal Mushrooms:** Such as chaga and cordyceps, activate the Nrf2 pathway, can lower adrenal stress, and promote autophagy.
*   **Metabolic State of Ketosis:** Achieved through fasting or a ketogenic diet.
    *   Ketosis -> PGC-1 alpha, AMPK, SIRT1 increase -> Mitochondria Biogenesis
    *   Ketone bodies, modulated by PGC-1 alpha, also exhibit anti-inflammatory effects. The ketogenic diet mimics many aspects of fasting.

These methods shift metabolic gears towards a fasted physiology, offering an experience that is metabolically similar to fasting without the need for prolonged food restriction.

## The Importance of Actual Fasting and Time-Restricted Eating

While the aforementioned hacks mimic fasted physiology, the unique characteristic of a 'pure fasted state'—metabolic suspension without digesting food, where the body is completely unstimulated by external nutrients—can only be achieved through actual fasting.

Therefore, practicing some form of time-restricted eating is beneficial for everyone, regardless of diet or goals.

**Evidence for Time-Restricted Eating:**
*   **Mouse Studies:** Fasting 16 hours and eating within an 8-hour window prevented mice from becoming obese and developing hypertension, even when fed a junk food diet.
*   **Human Studies:** Eating within a 6-hour window improved blood glucose levels, insulin sensitivity, lipid profile, circadian gene activation, and cardiometabolic risk factors compared to a standard three meals a day approach.

A daily 16-hour fast with an 8-hour (or shorter) eating window is considered optimal for digestion, circadian rhythms, and chronobiology. However, a daily 16-hour fast alone is generally insufficient to activate significant autophagy or other deep longevity pathways without incorporating the aforementioned "hacks" to accelerate the process.

While incorporating these strategies allows for faster benefits, longer fasts are still valuable and should be practiced occasionally. The goal is not always to fast more, but to optimize the fasting process, potentially fasting less while still reaping significant metabolic benefits.
